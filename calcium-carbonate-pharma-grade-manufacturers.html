The Product - Calcium Carbonate Pharma Grade

Calcium carbonate is an inactive chemical compound that is extracted from mineral rock foundations such as marble, limestone, etc. It is commonly found in rocks and is the main constituent of shells of marine animals, pearls, snails, and eggs. It is formed when calcium ions in hard water react with carbonate ions to create limescale. For medicinal purposes, it is used as a calcium supplement or as an antacid, but over-consumption could be harmful and lead to poor digestion.


It is also manufactured synthetically by passing carbon dioxide through hydrated lime to give a comparatively lighter product with lower bulk density.

Classifications of Calcium Carbonate

Calcium carbonate is classified into three polymorphic forms:-

Calcite:

It is most stable and least soluble in nature. It is broadly found in metamorphic, sedimentary, and igneous rocks. Calcite is a common component of sedimentary rocks like limestone and marble.

Aragonite:

It is less stable and more soluble in comparison to calcite. Biological and physical procedures like precipitation by freshwater and marine helps in the formation of aragonite. It is generally used for removing the pollutants like cobalt, zinc, and lead from polluted wastewaters.

Vaterite:

Vaterite is usually hexagonal in shape and is less stable than both the minerals calcite as well as aragonite but solubility is higher. It is majorly used in regenerative medicine and in personal care products.

General Properties of Calcium Carbonate

Physical Properties

  • The pure form of calcium carbonate is an odorless powder.
  • Color is super white
  • Density is 2.71 g/mL and melting point is 1,339 °C in calcite form
  • Aragonite has a density of 2.83 g/mL and a melting point of 825 °C.

Chemical Properties

  • Insoluble in water
  • Stable at normal temperature
  • At very high temperatures, when calcium carbonate is heated, it decomposes to form calcium oxide, releasing carbon dioxide.
