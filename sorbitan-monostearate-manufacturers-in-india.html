Sorbitan Monostearate - Product Description

Sorbitan monostearate is a synthetic ester made by the reaction of Sorbitan with stearic acid under controlled conditions. SMS is used in the manufacture of food and healthcare products as a non-ionic surfactant with emulsifying, dispersing, and wetting properties. It is also employed to create synthetic fibers, metal machining fluid, and as a brightener in the leather industry. It is used in yeast manufacturing to protect the yeast from excess drying and also helps rehydrate the yeast cells.


Sorbitan monostearate (abbreviation SMS), or Span 60, is an emulsifier esterified from Sorbitan and stearic acid with the European food additive number E491. This ingredient is mostly used in baking yeast by improving the activity of instant dry yeast when the yeast is rehydrated before use.

Uses of Sorbitan monostearate

As leading suppliers and exporters of Sorbitan Monostearate in the world, we have been serving many foods and other industries with absolute consistency in quality at a reasonable cost.

Food Applications

Sorbitan monostearate or SMS is used as an emulsifier, commonly together with polysorbates to keep water and oil mixed in many food products.
It is commonly used in instant fat and oil emulsions, desserts, food supplements, cake, spread, margarine, coffee whiteners.

  • Dry yeast: maintain the moisture, increase the shelf life of dry yeast, and help the rehydration of yeast cells before blended with other dough ingredients.
  • Ice cream: promote emulsification of dairy fat, prevent the formation of ice crystals, and improve the mouthfeel.
  • Margarine: improve emulsion stability and reduce sandiness.
  • Whipping cream: improve foam volume and contribute to a nice and stiff foam.
  • Bread & Cake: increase the loaf volume and improve the texture of bread and cake.
  • Confections and chocolate: stabilize the emulsion of oils and fats.
  • Flavored fermented milk products.
  • Beverage whiteners.
